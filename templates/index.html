Tools for analyzing and visualizing data of silicon absorption in Vietnamese rice varieties

Welcome to the cutting-edge bioinformatics web tool designed for analyzing and visualizing sequence data related to silicon absorption in Vietnamese rice varieties. Our website offers user-friendly interfaces for seamless data processing and insightful visualization, enhancing research efficiency.

Our Story

The Problem: Understanding the genetic mechanisms governing silicon (Si) uptake in rice is important for improving agricultural productivity and sustainability. Current tools for Si identification are fragmented, complex, and require significant computational expertise, slowing research progress.

Our Solution: We are developing integrated, user-friendly software tools to automate data processing tasks related to Si uptake in rice. These tools streamline workflows minimizing labor and errors and have a user-friendly interface.

TASSEL

TASSEL 5.0 is crucial in our bioinformatics project on processing data of silicon absorption in Vietnamese rice. It excels in genetic analysis, offering tools for efficient data processing and visualization. Using its command line interface, we manage large-scale genetic data, perform complex statistical analyses like MLM to identify key genetic markers and generate Q-Q plots and Manhattan plots. This is very useful in visualizing data into diagram that help users easily get accurate results.
